Working


Diabetes is a chronic condition in which the body develops a resistance to insulin, a hormone which converts food into glucose. Diabetes affect many people worldwide and is normally divided into Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes. Both have different characteristics. Here in this web application, we analyze the PIMA Indian Diabetes dataset (view dataset) to predict if a particular is at a risk of developing diabetes, given the independent factors.

A lot of research has happened on the non-invasive automated detection of diabetes using machine learning techniques. Machine learning was employed based on steps of feature extraction, feature selection and classification.


Here we use the Random Forest Classification to do so.

About Random Forest Classification

Random forest, like its name implies, consists of a large number of individual decision trees (to know more about decision trees click here) that operate as an ensemble. Each individual tree in the random forest spits out a class prediction and the class with the most votes becomes our model’s prediction. The fundamental concept behind random forest is a simple but powerful one — the wisdom of crowds. Random forests or random decision forests are an ensemble learning method for classification, regression and other tasks that operate by constructing a multitude of decision trees at training time and outputting the class that is the mode of the classes or mean/average prediction of the individual trees.
